About the project

On October 30, 2020, the minister of Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship, tabled the 2021‒2023 Immigration Levels Plan, which sets out a path for responsible increases to immigration targets to help the Canadian economy recover from COVID-19, drive future growth and create jobs for middle class Canadians.*


We are committed to helping would-be immigrants explore more about Canada. Use these Web charts to enhance your social studies, history, geography and Canadian studies! You can explore the Web charts to understand which countries the immigrants came from and the details (Occupation, wage&salary and house price) of your destination. Explore them today!

Where did the immigrants come from?

Instruction

This world map shows the source of Canada’s immigrants. Specifically, it includes the names of the source countries and the percentage of immigrants respectively. The "immigrants" are the new permanent resident (PR) visa holders in Canada for a given period of time (year or quarter) from the data source. You can search for a specific source country in the dropdown box on the top right.


Data Source: https://open.canada.ca/data/en/dataset/f7e5498e-0ad8-4417-85c9-9b8aff9b9eda

Where can you go and what's your future wage?

Instruction

The chart below shows the potential industry that you could enter by National Occupational Classification (NOC) based on historical data. Choose the province and occupation you're interested in, the left chart shows the general industry of the occupation. The list on the right displays the average wage in each province. The list is ranked based on the immigrant percentage and then the wages.


Data Source:

  • https://www.jobbank.gc.ca/trend-analysis/job-market-reports
  • https://open.canada.ca/data/en/dataset/f7e5498e-0ad8-4417-85c9-9b8aff9b9eda

    • How's the wage of your NOC and the house price?

    Instruction

    The map below represents the average wage in each province by National Occupational Classification (NOC) in Canada. Select one NOC in the dropwown box on the left, the map would show the wage in each province of your NOC. The higher the wage is, the darker of the color saturation is. When hovering on the province, the house price in 2019 and 2020, and the year over year growth rate (YOY) are also dispalyed in the tooltip.


    Data Source:

  • https://www.jobbank.gc.ca/trend-analysis/job-market-reports
  • https://www.crea.ca/housing-market-stats/national-price-map/

    • How much does the housing cost?

    Instruction

    This chart represents the cities with top housing market prices in each province for 2019 and 2020. The year over year growth rate is showed in tooltip.


    Data Source: https://www.crea.ca/housing-market-stats/national-price-map/
