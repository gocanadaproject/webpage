Where did the immigrants come from?

Instruction

This world map shows the source of Canadaâ€™s immigrants. Specifically, it includes the names of the source countries and the percentage of immigrants respectively. The "immigrants" are the new permanent resident (PR) visa holders in Canada for a given period of time (year or quarter) from the data source. The list of source countries and immigrant percentage indicates which country of origin the primary source is and is ranked in descending order by number.

How's the wage of your NOC?

Instruction

The map below represents the average wage in each province by National Occupational Classification (NOC) in Canada. Select one NOC in the checkbox on the right, the map would show the wage in each province of your NOC. The higher the wage is, the darker of the color saturation is.

Where can you go and what's your future wage?

Instruction

The chart below shows the potential industry that you could enter by NOCs based on historical data. Choose the occupation you're interested in, the left shows the general industry of the occupation. The right list displays the total percentage of the immigrants in this occupation and the average wage in each province. The list is ranked based on the immigrant percentage and then the wages.

Instruction
