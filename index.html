About the project

On October 30, 2020, the minister of Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship, tabled the 2021‒2023 Immigration Levels Plan, which sets out a path for responsible increases to immigration targets to help the Canadian economy recover from COVID-19, drive future growth and create jobs for middle class Canadians.*


We are committed to helping would-be immigrants explore more about Canada. Use these Web charts to enhance your social studies, history, geography and Canadian studies! You can explore the Web charts to understand which countries the immigrants came from and the details (Occupation, wage&salary and house price) of your destination. Explore them today!

Where did the immigrants come from?

Instruction

This world map shows the source of Canada’s immigrants. Specifically, it includes the names of the source countries and the percentage of immigrants respectively. The "immigrants" are the new permanent resident (PR) visa holders in Canada for a given period of time (year or quarter) from the data source. The list of source countries and immigrant percentage indicates which country of origin the primary source is and is ranked in descending order by number.


Data Source: https://open.canada.ca/data/en/dataset/f7e5498e-0ad8-4417-85c9-9b8aff9b9eda

Where can you go and what's your future wage?

Instruction

The chart below shows the potential industry that you could enter by NOCs based on historical data. Choose the occupation you're interested in, the left shows the general industry of the occupation. The right list displays the total percentage of the immigrants in this occupation and the average wage in each province. The list is ranked based on the immigrant percentage and then the wages.


Data Source:

  • https://www.jobbank.gc.ca/trend-analysis/job-market-reports
  • https://open.canada.ca/data/en/dataset/f7e5498e-0ad8-4417-85c9-9b8aff9b9eda

    • How's the wage of your NOC and the house price?

    Instruction

    The map below represents the average wage in each province by National Occupational Classification (NOC) in Canada. Select one NOC in the checkbox on the right, the map would show the wage in each province of your NOC. The higher the wage is, the darker of the color saturation is. When hovering on the province, the house price is also dispalyed in the tooltip.


    Data Source:

  • https://www.jobbank.gc.ca/trend-analysis/job-market-reports
  • https://www.crea.ca/housing-market-stats/national-price-map/
